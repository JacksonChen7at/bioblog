How Plants Keep Us Alive 🌎

🌱 Introduction

Plants are essential to life on Earth. They provide oxygen, food, and support ecosystems. Without plants, life would not exist.

Learn Why Plants Matter

🌍 Why Plants Matter

Oxygen: Plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis.

Food: Plants are the base of the food chain.

Wildlife: Animals depend on plants for shelter.

Climate: Plants absorb carbon dioxide and help fight climate change.

🌿 Bryophyte: Moss

Scientific Name: Bryophyta

Mosses are small plants that grow in damp areas and absorb water through their leaves.

Fun Fact: Moss can survive extreme environments like the Arctic!

🌿 Pteridophyte: Fern

Scientific Name: Pteridophyta

Ferns reproduce using spores instead of seeds.

Fun Fact: Ferns existed over 360 million years ago!

🧠 Quick Quiz

1. What process do plants use to make oxygen?

Answer: Photosynthesis

2. What type of plant is moss?

Answer: Bryophyte

3. How do ferns reproduce?

Answer: Spores

🔚 Conclusion

Plants are essential for life. They provide oxygen, food, and help maintain Earth's climate. We must protect them.
